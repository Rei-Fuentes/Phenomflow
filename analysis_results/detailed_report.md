# Phenomenological Analysis: Structural Synthesis

**Date**: Sat Dec  6 23:52:41 CET 2025
**Interviews Analyzed**: 9

## 1. Universal Temporal Structure (Common Phases)

All participants traversed these common phases:

### 1. Initial Immersion
The moment of entering the VR environment and the immediate bodily/emotional reaction.

### 2. The Climax (The Jump)
The peak experience of looking down and making the decision to jump or step back.

### 3. Resolution
The aftermath of the experience, characterized by a return to baseline or lingering affect.

---

## 2. Experiential Profiles & Evolution

Participants were classified into distinct structures based on how they experienced the common phases:

### The Open-to-the-World Profile
**Description**: Characterized by curiosity, visual connection, and expansive bodily sensations.

**Evolution through Phases**:
- **1. Initial Immersion**: Curiosity, visual scanning, feeling of lightness.
- **2. The Climax (The Jump)**: Excitement, 'Chest-Opening', desire to fly/jump.
- **3. Resolution**: Sense of peace, connection with the virtual world.

**Participants**: P01, P04, P06, P08

### The Self-Absorbed Profile
**Description**: Characterized by rumination, internal focus, and bodily tension/contraction.

**Evolution through Phases**:
- **1. Initial Immersion**: Disorientation, 'Tension-SolarPlexus', internal questioning.
- **2. The Climax (The Jump)**: Fear, 'Contraction', dissociation or freezing.
- **3. Resolution**: Relief, return to self, lingering anxiety.

**Participants**: P02, P03, P05, P07
